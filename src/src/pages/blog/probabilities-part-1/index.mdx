## Definitions

A **random experiment** is an experiment <Katex formulae='\varepsilon' /> for which, with the same conditions,
each repetition ends up with many possible results and from which we cannot infere its result in advance.

The **space of possible results** of this experiment is called **state space** <Katex formulae='\Omega' />.

One **result** of the experiment is <Katex formulae='\omega \in \Omega' />. Results may be called achievements.

> Examples:
> * Coin flipping: <Katex formulae='\Omega = \{\text{head},\text{tail}\} \hArr \Omega = \{0,1\}' />
> * Dice throwing: <Katex formulae='\Omega = \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}' />
> * Lifetime of a bacterium: <Katex formulae='\Omega = \lbrack 0, +\infty \lbrack' />
> * Dart on an target of 30cm ø: <Katex formulae='\Omega = \{(x, y), \sqrt{x^2+y^2} \le 15 \}' />
> * Stock price of a financial asset on a time interval <Katex formulae='\lbrack t_1, t_2 \rbrack' />: <Katex formulae='\Omega = S(\lbrack t_1, t_2 \rbrack, \reals_+)' />

A **random event** *A* associated to <Katex formulae='\varepsilon' /> is a subset of <Katex formulae='\Omega' />. <Katex formulae='\textcolor{red}A \subset \Omega' />:

> Examples:
> * Coin flipped is head: <Katex formulae='A = \{0\}' />
> * For *n* coin flipped, <Katex formulae='\Omega = \{0, 1\}^n' />, <Katex formulae='\omega = (\omega_1, \dots ,\omega_n)' />,
    the number of head is over or equal to the number of face:
    <Katex formulae='A = \{ \omega \in \Omega, \sum^n_{i=1} \omega _i \ge \frac{n}{2} \}' />

For *A* and *B*, 2 events of <Katex formulae='\Omega' />, so that <Katex formulae='\forall A,B \in \Omega' />, we can say:
* `not`: *A* isn't reached: <Katex formulae='\textcolor{red}{A^c} = \{ \omega \in \Omega \mid \omega \notin A \}' /> (aka A complementary or A inverse):

* `and`: *A* and *B* are reached: <Katex formulae='\textcolor{red}{A \cap B} = \{ \omega \in \Omega \mid \omega \in A \text{ and } \omega \in B \}' /> (aka the intersection):

* `or`: *A* or *B* are reached: <Katex formulae='\textcolor{red}{A \cup B} = \{ \omega \in \Omega \mid \omega \in A \text{ or } \omega \in B \}' /> (aka the union):

* `imply`: *A* reached implies *B* reached: <Katex formulae='A_\text{reached} \rArr B_\text{reached} ~\text{then}~ A \subset B' />.
* `incompatible`: *A* and *B* are incompatible (incongruous, conflicting, ...): <Katex formulae='A \cap B = \empty' />.
* `always true`: <Katex formulae='\Omega' /> is the certain event: all results of the experiment take their value into it.
* `always false`: <Katex formulae='\empty' /> is the impossible event.

These operations are associative and commutative:
<Katex displayMode formulae='\begin{aligned} A \cup B = B \cup A &\text{ ; } A \cap B = B \cap A \\ (A \cup B) \cup C = A \cup (B \cup C) &\text{ ; } (A \cap B) \cap C = A \cap (B \cap C) \\ \end{aligned}' />
They also have the following properties:
<Katex displayMode formulae='\begin{aligned} A \cup A = A &~~;~~ A \cap A = A \\ A \cup \empty = A ~~;~~ A \cap \empty = \empty &~~;~~ A \cup \Omega = \Omega ~~;~~ A \cap \Omega = A \\ A \cup A^c = \Omega &~~;~~ A \cap A^c = \empty  \end{aligned}' />
They are distributive:

* <Katex formulae='\color{red} A \cap (B \cup C) = (A \cap B) \cup (A \cap C)' />:
